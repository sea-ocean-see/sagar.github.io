Modular Enhanced Therapeutic Delivery System (METDS)

This is my research project under Dr.Garret Casey which focuses on the design and development of a modular cell delivery system (MCDS) to enhance cancer treatment efficacy. We aim to create a targeted drug delivery system that can selectively deliver high concentrations of therapeutics to cancer cells while minimizing off-target toxicities. By employing receptor-mediated endocytosis and utilizing vitamin transporters as entry ligands, we achieve precise targeting and improved bioavailability. The modularity of our system allows for the integration of various therapeutic agents, diagnostics, and drug resistance modulators, expanding the therapeutic window and addressing drug resistance. Our first-generation MCDS includes different fluorophore-labeled constructs like Rohodoamine,coumarin and FITC to study uptake, optimize delivery concentrations, and assess cellular toxicity in various cancer cell lines. This approach aims to advance cancer treatment by increasing the potency and selectivity of chemotherapeutic agents.
